Activity 2: Hybrid Solar PV and Wind Energy System for a Rural Health Clinic

Figure 2 â€” Hybrid Renewable Energy System Architecture
Source of Energy: This system combines two renewable energy sources â€” solar radiation and wind energy â€” to provide reliable electricity for a rural health clinic. Solar photovoltaic (PV) panels convert sunlight into electrical energy, while a small wind turbine harnesses kinetic energy from the wind to generate additional power.

Conversion Process: The DC electricity generated by both solar panels and the wind turbine passes through charge controllers to maintain battery health and regulate energy flow. The stored DC energy in the battery bank is then converted to AC using an inverter, supplying power to essential medical equipment and lighting within the clinic.

Output/Utilization: The hybrid system ensures continuous electricity supply even during cloudy or windless periods, supporting refrigeration for vaccines, lighting, water purification units, and communication devices.

Real-World Relevance: Such hybrid renewable systems are vital for rural and remote healthcare centers where grid connectivity is unreliable or unavailable. They enhance energy resilience, reduce dependency on diesel generators, and promote sustainable community healthcare operations.

Activity 3: Wave Energy Conversion System for Coastal Desalination Plant

Figure 3 â€” Wave Energy Conversion + Desalination Architecture
Source of Energy: The system utilizes ocean wave energy available along coastal regions as a renewable power source. The continuous motion of waves provides mechanical energy that can be harvested through floating buoy systems or oscillating water columns positioned near the shore.

Conversion Process: The mechanical motion generated by wave action drives hydraulic pistons or air turbines connected to an electric generator, converting the kinetic energy of waves into electrical energy. This electricity powers a desalination plant, typically using the reverse osmosis process to separate salt from seawater and produce freshwater.

Output/Utilization: The system simultaneously generates clean electricity and provides a sustainable freshwater supply for nearby coastal communities, reducing dependence on grid electricity or fossil-fuel-based desalination.

Real-World Relevance: Wave-powered desalination offers a practical solution for addressing water scarcity and energy challenges in coastal areas. It promotes the use of marine renewable resources, contributing to sustainable development and environmental conservation.
