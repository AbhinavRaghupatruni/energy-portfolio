Activity 1 — Solar Water Heating

2000 L/day | Hostel

Source of Energy: Solar radiation captured by rooftop collectors.

Conversion Process: Heat transfer fluid carries heat to an insulated tank via exchanger. The solar water heating system designed for a university hostel with a daily hot water demand of 2000 liters consists of the following major components: solar collectors, storage tank, heat exchanger, piping network, and auxiliary heater. Solar energy is captured by flat plate collectors or evacuated tube collectors installed on the rooftop. These collectors absorb incident solar radiation and convert it into thermal energy. A working fluid (typically water or glycol mixture) circulates through the collectors, transferring absorbed heat to a heat exchanger located in the insulated storage tank. The heated water is stored in the tank, maintaining its temperature through proper insulation. During cloudy days or low radiation periods, an auxiliary electric or gas heater ensures an uninterrupted hot water supply. The control valves and sensors regulate the flow rate and maintain the desired temperature automatically. The hot water distribution system supplies water to bathrooms, kitchens, and laundry sections throughout the hostel.

Output: Hot water for bathing and kitchen use.

Activity 2 — Hybrid PV + Wind System

Rural Health Clinic

Source of Energy: Solar panels and wind turbine hybrid setup.

Conversion Process: Power stored in batteries and inverted to AC for clinic use.The hybrid renewable energy system integrates solar photovoltaic (PV) panels and a wind turbine to provide reliable, continuous electricity for a rural health clinic where grid access is limited or unavailable. The conversion process begins when the solar PV panels convert sunlight directly into direct current (DC) electricity using the photovoltaic effect. Simultaneously, the wind turbine captures kinetic energy from moving air and converts it into mechanical energy, which is then transformed into electrical energy through a generator.

Output: Reliable energy for lights, vaccines, and communication devices.

Activity 3 — Wave Energy + Desalination

Coastal Freshwater Supply

Source of Energy: Mechanical energy from ocean waves.

Conversion Process: Converts wave motion into power for desalination units.The wave energy conversion system utilizes the continuous motion of ocean waves to generate electricity that powers a desalination plant for freshwater production. The process begins with wave capture devices, such as oscillating water columns (OWC) or floating buoys, which harness the kinetic and potential energy of ocean waves. In the mechanical-to-electrical conversion stage, the motion of waves drives hydraulic pistons, air turbines, or linear generators, converting the mechanical energy into rotational motion. This rotation powers an electric generator, producing electrical energy. The generated electricity is conditioned using power converters and controllers to maintain stable output suitable for desalination operations.

Output: Produces clean water and electricity for coastal regions.
